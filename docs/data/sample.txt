## Paradocs

| **A Paragraph-Oriented Document Presentation System**
---
Paradocs is a paragraph-oriented text presentation system.
It is suitable for presenting a large piece of text 📃 , typically paragraph by paragraph, while making comments and explanations.
With Paradocs, each press of a key ⌨️ or button 🖱 highlights one sentence after another 🖍.
This allows the audience to know which particular sentence the presenter is focusing on at the moment.
---
### Types of Blocks
---
#### Regular Paragraph

This is text in a regular paragraph.
In a normal paragraph, pressing a key will move the highlight from sentence to sentence.
Sentences in regular text are highlighted 🖍 when focused.
They are printed in grey when not focused.
The slide frame automatically scrolls when the text cannot fit on it at the same time.

---
#### Static Paragraph

Below is an example of a static paragraph.
When creating a static paragraph, add a vertical bar symbol at the beginning of the line.

| This is static paragraph.
| Sentences in static text are not highlighted.
| They are always printed in black.

---
#### Unordered List

The unordered list means the so-called bullet point ⚫ format.
To describe the elements of an unordered list, put an asterisk and a space at the beginning of each line.
The list scrolls automatically when all the elements don't fit on the slide.

* Unordered list item 1
* Unordered list item 2
* Unordered list item 3
---
#### Ordered List (Numeric)

In an ordered list, the items in the list will be numbered automatically 🔢 .

1. Numeric list item 1
2. Numeric list item 2
3. Numeric list item 3
---
#### Ordered List (Alphabetical)

Each of the items in the list can be given a letter of the alphabet 🔤 .

a. Alphabetical list item 1
b. Alphabetical list item 2
c. Alphabetical list item 3
---
#### Table

You can create tables using standard Markdown syntax.
All lines must start and end with a pipe character.

| Name  | Score | Grade |
|-------|-------|-------|
| Alice | 95    | A     |
| Bob   | 87    | B+    |
| Carol | 72    | C     |

### Text Decoration and Annotation
---
#### Text Decoration

Text segments can be bolded, italicized, underlined, or highlighted.

* This is *itallic*
* This is **bold**
* This is _underlined_
* This is ==highlighted==
---
#### Showing Notes

It is possible to display short text in notes 📔 that are separated from the body text.
For example, when you're highlighting this sentence, you'll see a note at the bottom.{note: This is the note text}
The note text can be dragged across the screen with the mouse.{note: Try dragging this box!}

#### Quiz

You can create a quiz 🙋 with which you initially keep the text hidden from your audience and reveal it at the appropriate time.

| * In static text block, words and phrases in {curly brackets}
| * are rendered with {overlaying box} elements
| * so that you can {create a quiz} for the audience
---
#### Multiple Choice Quiz

You can create a clickable multiple choice quiz.
Mark the correct answer with an asterisk before the option letter.

| {mcq: What is the capital of France?
|   a) London
|   b) Berlin
|   *c) Paris
|   d) Rome
| }

To embed a image to the slide, you can specify the URL of an image file in PNG/JPG/GIF format uploaded on the web and display it on the slide 🖼 .

### Additional Features
---
#### Text-to-Speech Read-Aloud

Sentences in regular paragraphs can be played back with a text-to-speech engine 🗣 available in your browser.
The available languages and audio variations vary by OS and browser.

When TTS is available for highlighted content, a speaker icon 🔈 appears in the top left corner of the presentation screen.
Click on this icon or press the period key to play the TTS voice.
During playback, the word currently being spoken is highlighted with a yellow background.

---
#### Automatic Sentence Splitting

!! You can use the automatic sentence splitting feature. Just prefix the text with double exclamation marks. Each sentence must end with a period or other punctuation mark. Paradocs will split the text into separate sentences automatically.
---
#### Sticky Note

You can show your audience what comes to mind during your presentation by writing it in a sticky note.

You can show or hide the sticky note by clicking its icon 📃 at the top left corner of the presentation screen.
The contents of the sticky note will be retained even if the slide changes.
---
#### Automatic Presentation

To make a fully automatic presentation, click on the magic wand icon 🧙 in the upper left hand corner of the screen.
From the current or next fragment to be highlighted to the final fragment of the entire presentation, text read-aloud, video playback, etc. will be performed automatically.
You can have your computer make a whole presentation for you 🤖 ❗
